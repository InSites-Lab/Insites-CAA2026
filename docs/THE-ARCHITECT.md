# The Architect — Build Your Own AI Agent

**The Architect** is a meta-agent that helps you design AI system instructions for any domain. Instead of starting from a blank page, you describe what you want your agent to do, and The Architect proposes a structured draft that you refine through conversation.

This is the core activity in the workshop's **Ethics in Practice** session — participants who have just experienced a structured Human-AI collaboration (CBSA) now build their own agent, applying the design principles they encountered.

---

## How to Use It

1. **Choose your platform:** Claude.ai, ChatGPT, or Google Gemini
2. **Paste the instructions:** Copy the contents of [`agent-for-agents-en.md`](../InSites-Brain/agent-for-agents/agent-for-agents-en.md) into the system prompt (Claude Project Instructions, GPT Instructions field, or Gemini system instructions)
3. **Start a conversation:** Describe what kind of agent you want to build
4. **The Architect proposes a draft** — you critique, refine, and iterate

A Hebrew version is also available: [`agent-for-agents-he.md`](../InSites-Brain/agent-for-agents/agent-for-agents-he.md)

---

## The 5-Step Protocol

| Step | What happens |
|------|-------------|
| 1. **Rapid Intake** | You describe your idea. The Architect skips generic questions and moves straight to drafting. |
| 2. **The Proposal** | A structured draft: role definition, simulation experts, logic flow, and a menu of design principles to include (Evidence Mandate, Citation Discipline, Human-in-the-Loop, Transparency, Progressive Disclosure). |
| 3. **Your Feedback** | You review the draft — what works, what doesn't, what's missing. |
| 4. **Iteration** | The Architect refines based on your feedback. Repeat as needed. |
| 5. **Finalization** | A clean, ready-to-paste set of system instructions for your agent. |

---

## Key Ideas

- **Content before control:** It's easier to critique a draft than to design from scratch. The Architect builds first, then you shape it.
- **Simulator mindset:** The agent is designed as a function simulator, not a persona — it simulates expertise rather than pretending to *be* an expert.
- **Principles transfer:** The design principles you experienced during the CBSA assessment (evidence mandate, human-in-the-loop, transparency) become candidate constraints for your own agent.

---

## Workshop Context

After completing a CBSA heritage assessment with InSites-CAA, participants use The Architect to build an agent for their own professional domain. This connects the workshop's analytical experience to a transferable skill — designing AI systems that are transparent, grounded, and human-centered.
